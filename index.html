About PFAS

PFAS (Per- and Polyfluoroalkyl Substances) are synthetic chemicals known for their resistance to water, heat, and oil. Commonly used since the 1950s in everyday products like non-stick cookware, waterproof clothing, and firefighting foams, these chemicals are nearly indestructible due to their strong carbon-fluorine bonds.


Because PFAS don’t break down easily, they build up in the environment—contaminating soil, water, and even our bodies. In Africa, clay-rich soil used for construction has been exposed to PFAS through industrial waste, turning a vital resource into a toxic threat.,



More about us

We’re tackling this crisis by transforming PFAS-contaminated clay into clean, durable bricks using high-temperature thermal remediation. Our mission is to restore safe building practices, reduce environmental harm, and bring new life to affected communities.
